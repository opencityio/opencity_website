What is it?

Open City is all about digital, creative and ideas people in Peterborough coming together to tackle specific local challenges to make Peterborough a better place to live and work. Open City is part of Peterborough DNA, the programme of work creating a smarter, more sustainable city.


We don’t like to brag, but this is an exciting initiative and a bit of a first – community and grassroots led solutions to help the city think differently about how we approach challenges.


Open City’s first project is to design content for the interactive screen being installed on Long Causeway, Peterborough.

Frequently Asked Questions
How do I get involved ?

Simple, register on eventbrite (below) to attend the project inception at the Town Hall on 1st October 2014 7-9pm

What kind of skills do we need ?

It’s important that we have a cross section of people from around the city. Businesses, local attractions, heritage people, environmental people, cultural people will all be able to use the screen to promote products, services and events. We need designers to make sure the information is presented in a clear, informative way, developers who want to learn about new technology and work alongside other engineers to create a working solution, and finally, the end-users of the screen to let us know what kind of information would be useful.

Who is paying for the event ?

Open City is being supported as part of Peterborough DNA to encourage innovation and creativity across the city. In 2012 the government’s innovation agency, innovate UK (Technology Strategy Board), launched a UK-wide future cities competition to demonstrate how cities could take a new approach to how they run. By developing and testing innovative ideas, cities were called upon to shape smarter and more sustainable places. With a bid developed with public, private and third sector partnership, Peterborough came second and the ‘Peterborough DNA’ programme was born: to implement the ideas, innovations and new systems that were put forward.

Will I get paid for my time ?

In short, no. This is a community event with the opportunity for local people to shape cutting edge, innovative technology to support economic growth and innovation in Peterborough. It’s an opportunity to put your stamp on something exciting for local residents to enjoy and feel proud of Peterborough.
